Here's a time line of Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam's life:

  • 1931 - born on 15 October, Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu.
  • 1955 - Kalam moved to Madras to study aerospace engineering in MIT.
  • 1960 - joined the Aeronautical Development Establishment of the Defence Research and Development Organisation as a scientist after becoming a member of the DRDS.He started his career by designing a small hovercraft, but remained unconvinced by his choice of a job at DRDO. Kalam was also part of the INCOSPAR committee working under Vikram Sarabhai, the renowned space scientist.
  • 1963-1964 - visited NASA's Langley Research Center in Hampton, Virginia; Goddard Space Flight Center in Greenbelt, Maryland; and Wallops Flight Facility.
  • 1965 - Kalam had first started work on an expandable rocket project independently at DRDO
  • 1969 - received the government's approval and expanded the programme to include more engineers.
  • 1970 to 1980s - transferred to the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) where he was the project director of India's first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III) which successfully deployed the Rohini satellite in near-earth orbit in July.
  • 1992 - served as the Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister and Secretary of the Defence Research and Development Organisation from July 1992 to December 1999.
  • 1998 - along with cardiologist Soma Raju, Kalam developed a low cost coronary stent, named the "Kalam-Raju Stent".
  • 2002 - Kalam became the 11th President of India, succeeding K. R. Narayanan. His term lasted from 25 July 2002 to 25 July 2007.
  • 2011 - Kalam was criticised by civil groups over his stand on the Koodankulam Nuclear Power Plant; The protesters were hostile to his visit as they saw him as a pro-nuclear scientist and were unimpressed by the assurances he provided regarding the safety features of the plant.
  • 2012 - launched a programme for the youth of India called the What Can I Give Movement, with a central theme of defeating corruption.

  • 2015 - Kalam travelled to Shillong to deliver a lecture on "Creating a Livable Planet Earth" at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong.While giving the lecture he collapsed and died.
